What is an LLP?

A Limited Liability Partnership (LLP) is a hybrid business structure that combines the flexibility of a traditional partnership with the benefits of limited liability. Introduced by the LLP Act, 2008, it provides partners with protection from the wrongful acts of other partners.

LLP is ideal for professionals like CAs, Lawyers, Consultants, and small businesses that want a formal structure without the compliance burden of a Private Limited Company. Partners contribute to the LLP through capital and share profits as per the LLP Agreement.

Benefits of LLP

Limited Liability

Partners' personal assets are protected. Liability is limited to their contribution.

Minimal Compliance

Less annual filing requirements compared to Private Limited Companies.

No Minimum Capital

Start with any capital amount. No minimum contribution required.

Flexibility

Partners can define their own rules through LLP Agreement.

Tax Benefits

No Dividend Distribution Tax. Profits taxed at flat 30% rate.

Perpetual Succession

LLP continues to exist regardless of changes in partners.

Requirements for LLP Registration

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between LLP and Partnership Firm?

In a Partnership Firm, partners have unlimited liability and are personally liable for business debts. In an LLP, liability is limited to the capital contribution. LLP is a separate legal entity, while Partnership is not.

Can an LLP be converted to a Private Limited Company?

Yes, an LLP can be converted to a Private Limited Company under Section 366 of the Companies Act, 2013. The process involves filing specific forms with the ROC.

What are the annual compliance requirements for LLP?

LLPs must file Form 8 (Statement of Accounts) and Form 11 (Annual Return) every year. Additionally, Income Tax Return must be filed annually. LLPs with turnover above ₹40 lakhs or contribution above ₹25 lakhs need audit.

Is audit mandatory for all LLPs?

No, audit is mandatory only if the LLP's annual turnover exceeds ₹40 lakhs or partners' contribution exceeds ₹25 lakhs. Small LLPs below these limits are exempt from audit requirements.
