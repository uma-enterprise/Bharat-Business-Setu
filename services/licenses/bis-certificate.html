What is BIS Certificate?

BIS (Bureau of Indian Standards) is the national standards body of India that provides quality certification for products sold in India. The BIS certification (commonly known as ISI Mark) ensures that products meet the quality and safety standards set by the government.

Mandatory Products: BIS certification is mandatory for 370+ products including electronics, electrical appliances, cement, packaged water, and many more.

Types of BIS Certification Schemes

ISI Mark (Product Certification)

For Manufacturers

  • Mandatory for 370+ products
  • Factory inspection required
  • Quality testing at BIS labs
  • Annual surveillance
For Importers

FMCS (Foreign Manufacturer Certification)

For Imported Products

  • For foreign manufacturers
  • Factory inspection abroad
  • Indian importer required
  • Mandatory for electronics

CRS (Compulsory Registration)

For Electronics

  • For electronic products
  • Self-declaration based
  • Lab testing required
  • Quick registration

Products Requiring BIS Certification

Electrical Appliances

Switches, cables, wires, plugs, sockets, MCBs, fans, motors, etc.

Electronics

LED products, adapters, power banks, laptops, mobile phones, etc.

Building Materials

Cement, steel, plywood, PVC pipes, glass, tiles, etc.

Packaged Water

Packaged drinking water and mineral water bottles.

Automotive Parts

Helmets, safety glass, tyres, LPG cylinders, etc.

Toys & Safety Products

Toys, pressure cookers, stainless steel utensils, etc.
