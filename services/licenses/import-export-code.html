What is Import Export Code (IEC)?

Import Export Code (IEC) is a 10-digit unique identification number issued by the Directorate General of Foreign Trade (DGFT), Ministry of Commerce, Government of India. It is mandatory for any business or individual who wants to import or export goods and services from India.

Lifetime Validity: IEC has lifetime validity and does not require renewal. However, annual updation on DGFT portal is now mandatory.

Benefits of IEC Registration

Global Market Access

Export your products and services to any country in the world.

Government Benefits

Avail export incentives, MEIS, SEIS and other government schemes.

Lifetime Validity

No renewal required. Once obtained, IEC is valid for lifetime.

Service Exports

Required for receiving foreign currency for IT and other service exports.

Bank Transactions

Mandatory for sending/receiving foreign currency through banks.

Customs Clearance

Required for customs clearance of import/export consignments.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is IEC mandatory for all exports?

Yes, IEC is mandatory for export of goods. For service exports, it's required to receive foreign currency payments. Some exemptions exist for personal use imports.

Does IEC expire?

No, IEC has lifetime validity. However, annual updation on DGFT portal is now mandatory (typically between April-June each year).

Can an individual get IEC?

Yes, individuals can obtain IEC for import/export activities. They need to apply using their individual PAN card.

Can I export without IEC?

No, you cannot export goods without IEC. For services, you won't be able to receive foreign currency payments through banks without IEC.
