What is Trademark Assignment?

Trademark Assignment is the process of transferring ownership of a registered trademark from one entity (Assignor) to another (Assignee). Unlike licensing, assignment involves complete transfer of rights, including the right to sue for infringement.

Assignment can be done for registered trademarks, pending applications, or even unregistered trademarks. It can be with or without the goodwill of the business.

Types of Trademark Assignment

Complete Assignment

Full transfer of all rights including goodwill for all goods/services.

Partial Assignment

Transfer for only specific goods/services, retaining rights for others.

With Goodwill

Transfer includes business reputation associated with the trademark.

Without Goodwill

Transfer of trademark rights only, excluding business goodwill.

Documents Required

From Assignor (Seller)

  • TM Registration Certificate
  • ID & Address Proof
  • NOC for Assignment
  • Authorization Letter

From Assignee (Buyer)

  • ID & Address Proof
  • Incorporation Certificate (if company)
  • Authorization Letter
  • Payment Proof

Deed Execution

Frequently Asked Questions

Can a pending trademark application be assigned?

Yes, both registered trademarks and pending applications can be assigned. The assignment application should include the TM application number.

What is the difference between assignment and licensing?

Assignment is complete transfer of ownership, while licensing is granting permission to use the trademark. In assignment, the original owner loses all rights; in licensing, they retain ownership.

Is stamp paper required for assignment deed?

While not mandatory for TM Registry, it's recommended to execute the assignment deed on stamp paper for legal validity. Stamp duty varies by state.
