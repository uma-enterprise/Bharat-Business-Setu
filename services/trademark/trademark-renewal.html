What is Trademark Renewal?

Trademark Renewal is the process of extending the validity of your registered trademark. In India, a trademark is valid for 10 years from the date of application. To maintain your trademark rights, you must renew it before expiry.

Important: If you fail to renew, your trademark will be removed from the Register, and anyone can apply to register your brand name!

When Should You Renew?

6 Months Before Expiry

You can file for renewal up to 6 months before your trademark expires.

Within 6 Months After Expiry

Late renewal possible with additional surcharge of ₹4,500.

Restoration (6-12 Months)

If missed, restoration is possible within 6-12 months after expiry.

After 12 Months

Trademark is removed from register. Fresh application needed.

Documents Required

Trademark Details

  • Trademark Registration Number
  • Copy of Registration Certificate
  • Date of Registration/Expiry

Owner Details

  • ID Proof of Owner
  • Address Proof
  • Authorization Letter (if agent filing)

Frequently Asked Questions

When should I renew my trademark?

You can apply for renewal anytime within 6 months before the expiry date. This ensures continuous protection without any gap.

What if I miss the renewal deadline?

You can still renew within 6 months after expiry with a late fee surcharge. After 6 months, you'll need to apply for restoration. After 12 months, the trademark is permanently removed.

How long is the renewed trademark valid?

Each renewal extends the trademark validity for 10 years. You can renew indefinitely, effectively providing lifetime protection.
