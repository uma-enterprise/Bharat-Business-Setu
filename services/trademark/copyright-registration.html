What is Copyright Registration?

Copyright is a legal right that protects original works of authorship such as literary works, music, software, art, films, and more. While copyright exists automatically upon creation, registration provides legal evidence of ownership and is essential for enforcing your rights in court.

Copyright protection in India lasts for the lifetime of the author plus 60 years, making it one of the longest-lasting IP protections available.

What Can Be Copyrighted?

Literary Works

Books, articles, blogs, poems, scripts, manuals, and databases.

Software & Apps

Computer programs, mobile apps, source code, and algorithms.

Music & Audio

Songs, compositions, sound recordings, and jingles.

Films & Videos

Movies, documentaries, YouTube videos, and animations.

Artistic Works

Paintings, photographs, sculptures, and graphic designs.

Architectural Works

Building designs, floor plans, and blueprints.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does copyright protection last?

For literary, dramatic, musical, and artistic works, copyright lasts for the lifetime of the author plus 60 years. For films and sound recordings, it's 60 years from publication.

Is registration mandatory for copyright protection?

No, copyright exists automatically upon creation. However, registration is highly recommended as it serves as legal proof of ownership and is essential for filing infringement lawsuits.

Can I copyright an idea?

No, copyright protects the expression of ideas, not the ideas themselves. The idea must be expressed in a tangible form - written down, recorded, or otherwise fixed in a medium.

How long does copyright registration take?

The registration process typically takes 2-3 months. There's a mandatory 30-day waiting period after filing, followed by examination by the Copyright Office.
